IT Work

What kind of work is done by the IT professional?

Most IT professionals start their day with looking at their emails and replying to them, then they usually have a daily stand-up meeting on what they will be working on throughout the day and what the manager is expecting from them by the end of the day. Sometimes they have meetings with the clients if they have any changes to the product they need, this is mostly their daily work, if we look at the start of the project, they have meetings with the product owner, analyst and other team members. Most of the time some new or junior developers work on resolving bugs and make documentation of the products too.

What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

Mostly during the day, they interact with their fellow developers, where they are working with junior and senior people, they also work with the data and business analyst, the product owner and analyst is mostly working with the clients and investors and the clients have their own team which is working with the customers. This is mainly for a client-based company. Product based companies have their own team to interact with the customers and explain their product to them.

Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

During the start of the project, they work on making user stories which means they break a large feature into small parts. Some of the developers also meet with the client to show the functionality of the product or if the client wants to see how the developer is working, he can meet with them too.

What aspect of their position is most challenging?

The most challenging part comes to a developer is to limit the use of additional services to keep the product under budget, they also must make sure the product is secure, at times there are moments they have to work on project that is premade and is not written by them in past, so understanding that can be a hassle too. Autonomous Vehicles

An autonomous vehicle is a vehicle that can drive itself from a starting point to a selected destination in a mode called “autopilot”, just like in aeroplanes. There are also various in-vehicle technologies and sensors, including adaptive cruise control, active steering (steer by wire), anti-lock braking systems (brake by wire), GPS navigation technology, lasers and radar.

What is the state of art of autonomous vehicles?

The state of art of autonomous vehicles is currently abstract. Autonomous vehicles are seen as a promising technology that has the potential to alter mobility and address a variety of traffic challenges, including accessibility, efficiency, convenience, and, most importantly, safety.
Vehicles propelled by internal combustion engines were used in the development of production automobiles. To this day, improvements in internal combustion technology are being made. The advancement of autonomous car technology is accelerating. Autonomous vehicle businesses have invested a significant amount of money in the development of autonomous vehicle technology with the goal of commercialising it.

What can be done now?

It can be said to be a self-driving car. To execute software and move between destinations without a human operator, it uses a combination of sensors, actuators, machine learning systems, and complicated and powerful algorithms. The sensors collect real-time information about the environment, such as geographic coordinates, vehicle speed and direction, acceleration, and potential obstructions. Car navigation is also possible since each vehicle is equipped with a global positioning system (GPS) and a geographic information system (GIS) that collects location data such as latitude and longitude. To determine the relative vehicle location, the location system employs an inertial navigation system (INS). Information concerning traffic and road facilities is stored on electronic maps. Path planning is generally accomplished by map matching, which determines the car's location. Three basic modalities are employed to perceive the environment: laser perception, optical perception, and radar perception.

What can be done in the future?

Recent advancements include crash alerts, earlier warnings about accidents, road construction, over-speed, traffic signals, fog warnings, the presence of black ice, and some location-based services, among others. These advancements, as well as advancements in connected vehicle technology can really help make autonomous vehicles fully commercial.

Autonomous vehicles must learn to recognize a variety of items in their route, ranging from branches and litter to animals and people. Tunnels that interfere with the Global Positioning System , building projects that necessitate lane changes, and difficult judgments, such as where to stop to allow emergency vehicles to pass, are all obstacles on the road.

The systems must make split-second decisions on when to slow down, swerve, or maintain regular acceleration. Self-driving cars have been reported pausing and swerving excessively when objects are identified in or near the highways, which is a continuing difficulty for developers. Not to mention the other problems that developers have to face.

Despite the fact that driverless car firms have made incredible progress in the technology, fully autonomous cars will not be available to the general public for many years. What is the likely impact?

The creation of autonomous vehicles can bring many benefits, along with all the benefits, it can also bring disadvantages to our society and impact some people in harmful ways. So here are some pros and cons for autonomous vehicles.

The likelihood of widespread usage of self-driving cars has a number of advantages, including a reduction in traffic accidents and the associated economic costs of property damage, injury, and death. These driverless vehicles will also save energy by maximising driving efficiency and reducing traffic congestion. The potential for net economic benefit is substantial. Most fatal car accidents are caused by human errors or bad decisions by humans such as drunk driving or distracted driving. Self-driving cars eliminate those risk variables from the equation, but they are still subject to other factors that cause crashes, such as mechanical faults.

Moreover, it can cause unintended consequences. Unemployment of vehicle drivers can happen as If cars, trucks, and buses start driving themselves, people who earn their living from driving these vehicles will suddenly find themselves out of a job. It will be difficult for these individuals to find new jobs fast, and the cost of retraining them may be prohibitive. One intriguing possibility is that in a few generations' hence, very few people would even know how to operate a car.

It can also affect the automobile industry. There’s a chance that people would increasingly rely on calling a driverless car from a shared fleet, similar to how they would call an Uber, driving a reduction in private car ownership. Why possess an expensive system that is prone to failure when you can just summon a self-driving car to take you anywhere you want, whenever you want? The automobile industry would be destroyed if private car ownership became obsolete, resulting in the loss of many employment, both directly and indirectly, as well as billions of dollars in economic activity.

Another problem with these autonomous vehicles is that hackers are able to take control of the automobiles. Security specialists have successfully hacked and taken control of a Tesla Model S and a Jeep Cherokee in the past, hunting for holes in modern autos. 67

Computer hardware and software would be in charge of a self-driving car. To take control of a car or even force it to crash on purpose, a malicious attacker may locate and exploit security weaknesses in any number of complicated systems.

What does a robot do?

Robot is any automatically operated machine that replaces human effort, though it may not resemble human beings in appearance or perform functions in a humanlike manner.

Robots help humans with dangerous jobs because they are capable of working in hazardous environments. They can handle lifting heavy loads, toxic substances and repetitive tasks that can last 24/7. This has helped companies to prevent many accidents, also saving time and money. For example, robots are used in industries to do simple repetitive tasks which are much more efficient.

Nanobots are robots that have been reduced down to microscopic size in order to execute a function in very small locations. Nanobots are still in the early stages of development. CHATTERBOT

Chatterbot is an artificial intelligence program that is used to replicate usual conversations a user would have with another person. It is usually used in situations where conversational pieces are required, an example would be customer service and would be in the form of either text or Text-To-Speech(TTS).

Chatterbot is a machine learning program which usually uses sophisticated AI, Natural Language Understanding (NLU) or extensive word processing while other chatterbots may use the method of searching a specific phrase to generate a response. Chatterbot is very common on websites whether it becomes like an assistant or just a FAQs platform. With its capability of NLU, it can process an input in natural language and give an appropriate response and would not need the developers to list all possible expressions the user may input. Along with NLU, chatterbot is able to hold contextual conversation which means that it does not only hold value in the current input but it also holds value in previous inputs and responses. As humans, contextual conversation is very common as we tend to be dependent on context to hold a proper conversation where both parties understand one another. Chatterbot today can memorise simple things like the user’s name, age or gender if it was mentioned but it still has trouble in memorising other things they may have talked about and plans so that the chatterbot would eventually be able to remember what it and the user may have talked about are currently being discussed and developed. Since some chatterbots are based on an AI, these chatterbots are able to learn from given contents or websites.

With that being said, these features would impact the industry greatly. With NLU, the bot would be able to create responses that would match the current context which would save time and money for developers as they would not need to list possible expressions and work on other segments to create a better program which would lead to better customer experience. Contextual conversation would also create a chatting environment in which the user would feel comfortable which would also lead to better customer experience. Having the chatterbot be able to utilise the data log and memorise what it and the user may have talked about and self-learn would be beneficial too as it would be able to adjust easily into the conversation and increase the quality of customer experience.

With the presence and the current continuous development of chatterbots, this would affect day-to-day tasks. As chatterbots are also available in a text or text-to-speech form, we would be able to ask and receive information easily. An example of a chatterbot that is popular and used often in today’s society is Google Assistant, this chatterbot is easily accessible as people would only need to say ‘Ok Google’ to activate it on their devices and from there they can ask or say anything. These chatterbots would save time for people and reduce risks that may occur as they would not need to take the time to fully focus on opening their device to get the information they need hence reducing risks that may occur when we are multitasking. An example of this would be trying to get directions via a GPS app like Google Maps or Apple Maps while driving, instead of having to risk getting into an accident by having to unlock our mobile phones, we would just need to say a certain phrase to activate the chatterbot and from there we can just ask the bot to get directions to our destination.
